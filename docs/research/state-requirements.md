# New York (NY)

**Verified 2026-06-19.**

### 1. Does NY license/regulate home inspectors? Which agency?
Yes. Home inspectors are licensed by the **New York State Department of State (NYS DOS)**, Division of Licensing Services, under the Home Inspection Professional License Law and 19 NYCRR Part 197.
Source: https://dos.ny.gov/home-inspector · https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

### 2. Pre-licensing education hours + breakdown
Applicants must complete **a course of study of not less than 140 hours** approved by the Department.
Source: https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

The 140 hours break down as **100 hours classroom + 40 hours field-based** (19 NYCRR § 197-2.3, "Subjects for study — home inspection"):

| Module | Topic | Hours |
|---|---|---|
| Module 1 | Structural / Exterior / Roof | 25 (classroom) |
| Module 2 | Interior / Insulation & Ventilation / Electrical | 25 (classroom) |
| Module 3 | Heating / Cooling / Plumbing | 25 (classroom) |
| Module 4 | Overview of Profession / NYS License Law / Report Writing | 25 (classroom) |
| Module 5 | Field-based training (unpaid, supervised) | 40 (field) |

Source (regulation text): https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/19-NYCRR-197-2.3
Source (140-hr requirement): https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

### 3. Required examination(s)
Applicants must pass **either** the NYS written examination **or** the National Home Inspector Examination (NHIE / EBPHI).
Source: https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector · https://nationalhomeinspectorexam.org/regulations/new-york/

### 4. Field / practical / ride-along training
Two pathways:
- **Education path:** within the 140 hours, **40 hours must be unpaid field-based inspections** in the presence of and under the direct supervision of a NYS-licensed home inspector, NYS-licensed professional engineer, or NYS-licensed architect; **or**
- **Experience path:** perform **not less than 100 home inspections** (paid or unpaid) under the direct supervision of a NYS-licensed home inspector, engineer, or architect.

Source: https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

### 5. Core topics/modules the curriculum must cover
From 19 NYCRR § 197-2.3: Structural; Exterior; Roof; Interior; Insulation & Ventilation; Electrical; Heating; Cooling; Plumbing; Overview of the Profession; NYS License Law; Report Writing; plus supervised field inspection.
Source: https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/new-york/19-NYCRR-197-2.3

### 6. Continuing education (CE)
**24 hours** of approved CE within each **two-year** license renewal period.
Source: https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

### Other NY notes
- High school diploma or equivalent required.
- Liability insurance required: minimum **$150,000 per occurrence / $500,000 aggregate**.
Source: https://dos.ny.gov/become-home-inspector

# Washington (WA)

**Verified 2026-06-19.**

> **Correction to the working assumption:** WA *was* suspected of having **no** state licensing for home inspectors. That is **incorrect.** Washington has licensed home inspectors since **July 1, 2009** under RCW 18.280.

### 1. Does WA license/regulate home inspectors? Which agency?
Yes. **Washington State Department of Licensing (DOL)** administers home-inspector licensing, with guidance from the **Home Inspector Advisory Licensing Board**, under **chapter RCW 18.280** (enacted via SB 6606).
Source: https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/home-inspectors/get-your-license-home-inspectors · https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true

### 2. Pre-licensing education hours + breakdown
A board-approved **120-hour "Fundamentals of Home Inspection" course** (RCW 18.280 requires "a minimum of one hundred twenty hours of classroom instruction approved by the board").
Source: https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/home-inspectors/get-your-license-home-inspectors · https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true

> The statute does **not** itemize a per-topic hour breakdown for the 120 hours; topic detail is set by board rule and by the SOP system list (see #5). **Flagged as uncertain** — hour-per-topic allocation not published in statute.

### 3. Required examination(s)
A written exam that is "psychometrically valid, reliable, and legally defensible." In practice it is delivered as a **state portion plus a national portion** (a national exam must include a WA-specific section).
Source: https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true · https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/home-inspectors/get-your-license-home-inspectors

### 4. Field / practical / ride-along training
**Up to 40 hours of field training** supervised by a licensed home inspector, **plus 5 actual inspections** (mentorship).
Source: https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/home-inspectors/get-your-license-home-inspectors · https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true

> **Conflict flagged:** the statute phrases this as "**up to** forty hours of field training," while the DOL "get your license" page and secondary sources describe **40 hours + 5 inspections** as the requirement. Treat "40 hours" as the ceiling and confirm the exact mandated minimum with DOL.

### 5. Core topics/modules the curriculum must cover
Per RCW 18.280, the inspection (and therefore the curriculum) covers: **roof; foundation; exterior; heating system; air-conditioning system; structure; plumbing; electrical systems;** and other aspects identified by the board, including **fire and safety hazards**.
Source: https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true

### 6. Continuing education (CE)
**24 hours** of board-approved CE **every two years**. Licenses are two-year terms expiring on the licensee's birthdate.
Source: https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=18.280&full=true · https://www.homeinspector.org/state-regulations/home-inspection-requirements-for-washington/

### Other WA notes
- Out-of-state inspectors licensed ≥2 of the last 4 years may sit only the state portion if their education meets WA standards.
Source: https://dol.wa.gov/professional-licenses/home-inspectors/get-your-license-home-inspectors

# Texas (TX)

**Verified 2026-06-19.**

### 1. Does TX license/regulate home inspectors? Which agency?
Yes. The **Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC)** licenses home inspectors under Texas Occupations Code Ch. 1102 and TAC Title 22, Part 23, Ch. 535. Three tiers: **Apprentice Inspector**, **Real Estate Inspector**, **Professional Real Estate Inspector**.
Source: https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/inspector

### 2. Pre-licensing education hours + breakdown
TREC education is tiered. Core qualifying modules (recur across tiers):

| Module | Hours |
|---|---|
| Property and Building Inspection Module I | 40 |
| Property and Building Inspection Module II | 40 |
| Analysis of Findings and Reporting Module | 20 |
| Business Operations and Professional Responsibility Module | 10 |
| Texas Law Module | 20 |
| Texas Standards of Practice Module | 24 |
| Texas Practicum (field training) | 40 |

- **Real Estate Inspector (substitute education/experience path):** **90 hours** core coursework (Modules I + II + Business Ops) plus Texas SOP Module (24) and Texas Practicum (40), plus 25 supervised inspections.
- **Professional Real Estate Inspector (substitute-experience path):** **194 hours** total = the 154 qualifying hours (all six modules above) **+ 40-hour Texas Practicum**.
- **Professional Real Estate Inspector (upgrade-from-RE-Inspector path):** **174 hours** + 12 months active experience + 175 inspections (Analysis/Reporting + Practicum not both required on this path).

Source (Professional): https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/professional-real-estate-inspector
Source (Real Estate Inspector): https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/real-estate-inspector
Source (overview): https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/inspector

> **2026 change flagged:** Effective **June 1, 2026**, TREC requires all online coursework and module exams to be completed **before** the Texas Practicum (field training). Confirm sequencing in the LMS.
> Source (secondary, confirm w/ TREC): https://icaschool.com/state-licensing/texas/

> **Uncertainty flagged:** The "154 hours" qualifying figure and the exact module composition per tier are easy to mis-attribute because TREC documents them per-pathway. The numbers above are taken from the TREC tier pages but the per-tier mapping should be re-confirmed directly with TREC before publishing.

### 3. Required examination(s)
A **national portion** + a **state (Texas) portion**, administered under contract with **PearsonVUE**. Coursework prerequisites apply before each portion.
Source: https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/professional-real-estate-inspector · https://nationalhomeinspectorexam.org/stateregulations/

> **Conflict flagged:** Some sources describe Texas's exam as "the NHIE." TREC's own pages describe a national + state portion delivered via PearsonVUE. Confirm whether the "national portion" *is* the NHIE or a TREC-specific national exam.

### 4. Field / practical / ride-along training
- **Texas Practicum: 40 hours** of field training (all tiers requiring practicum).
- **Real Estate Inspector path:** 25 real estate inspections under direct supervision.
- **Professional Inspector (experience-upgrade) path:** 12 months active experience + 175 inspections.
Source: https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/real-estate-inspector · https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/professional-real-estate-inspector

### 5. Core topics/modules the curriculum must cover
Driven by the TREC Standards of Practice (22 TAC §§ 535.227–535.231):
- **Structural Systems** (§535.228): foundations, grading & drainage, roof covering, roof structure & attic, walls/ceilings/floors, doors, windows, stairways, fireplaces/chimneys.
- **Mechanical Systems** (§535.229): appliances, cooling (HVAC), heating, ducts/vents/flues, plumbing systems.
- **Electrical Systems** (§535.230).
- **Optional Systems** (§535.231): e.g., pools/spas, outbuildings, etc.
Plus, from the qualifying modules: Analysis of Findings & **Reporting**, **Business Operations & Professional Responsibility (ethics)**, **Texas Law**, and **Texas Standards of Practice**.
Source: https://www.trec.texas.gov/forms/inspector-sop-printable-version · https://www.trec.texas.gov/become-licensed/professional-real-estate-inspector

### 6. Continuing education (CE)
**32 hours** of Inspector CE per **two-year** renewal period, which must include the **8-hour** Inspector Legal & Ethics + Standards of Practice Review course (commonly described as 4 hrs Legal & Ethics + 4 hrs SOP Review). No more than **16 hours** credit for any single subject.
Source: https://www.homeinspector.org/state-regulations/home-inspection-requirements-for-texas/ · https://www.trec.texas.gov/continuing-education-renewal-information · https://www.championsschool.com/home-inspection/tx/ce/

# Content gap analysis (across all three states)

**Already covered (reusable everywhere):** Electrical · Cooling & Heat Pumps · Heating · Insulation & Ventilation · Plumbing. These map cleanly onto NY-M2/M3, WA-M3/M4/M5/M6, and TX-M2.

**GAPS — content we must build:**
1. **Roofing systems** (covering + structure/attic) — required by all three states.
2. **Exterior** (cladding, grounds, grading & drainage) — all three.
3. **Structure & Foundations** — all three.
4. **Interior components** (walls/ceilings/floors, doors, windows, stairways, fireplaces) — all three.
5. **Appliances** — TX (Module II / §535.229) explicitly; relevant elsewhere.
6. **Report writing / Analysis of findings** — all three (TX has a dedicated 20-hr module + the TREC report form).
7. **Standards of Practice** — state-specific: TX §§535.227–535.231 (24-hr module), WA board SOP, NY SOP.
8. **Ethics / Professional responsibility / Business operations** — all three.
9. **State law** — fully state-specific: TX Texas Law module (20h), NY License Law, WA RCW 18.280.
10. **Fire & safety hazards** — explicitly called out by WA statute.
11. **Field/practicum delivery** — operational (mentorship logging, supervised-inspection tracking), not pure e-learning, for all three.

# Conflicts & uncertainties (consolidated)

1. **WA field training "40 hours."** Statute says "up to forty hours"; DOL page + secondary sources say "40 hours + 5 inspections." Confirm the binding minimum with DOL. (RCW 18.280; DOL page.)
2. **WA per-topic hour breakdown** for the 120-hour course is **not published in statute** — set by board rule. The per-topic allocation is unverified.
3. **TX exam = NHIE?** TREC describes a national + state portion via PearsonVUE; some third-party sources equate the national portion with the NHIE. Unconfirmed whether they are the same instrument.
4. **TX hour totals per tier (90 / 154 / 174 / 194).** These vary by pathway (substitute-education vs. apprenticeship vs. upgrade-from-RE-Inspector). The per-tier mapping is from TREC tier pages but should be re-confirmed directly with TREC, as it is easy to mis-attribute.
5. **TX June 1, 2026 sequencing rule** (coursework before practicum) is sourced to a school (ICA), not yet confirmed on a primary TREC page in this research — verify on trec.texas.gov.
6. **TX CE 8-hour legal/ethics+SOP split.** "32 hrs incl. 8-hr Legal & Ethics + SOP Review" is well-attested in secondary sources; the official inspector-CE page did not render the inspector-specific numbers in this research pass. Confirm against TAC §535 Subchapter R.
7. **NY 100/40 split.** The 100 classroom + 40 field split is confirmed in the regulation text (19 NYCRR §197-2.3) and corroborated; treated as solid, but note the "40 hours field" sits *inside* the 140 total (it is not an additional 40 on top).
